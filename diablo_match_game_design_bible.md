# Project Working Title

**Diablo Match** *(working title)*  
Pitch: **"Diablo II if it were a connect-3 game."**

A dark fantasy RPG where combat is resolved through an 8×8 match-3 board. Players explore an overworld map, fight enemies, gain loot, and customize their character through gear, abilities, and progression.

Players swap adjacent tiles to create matches of **3 or more**.

Possible matches:

- 3 in a row

# 6A. Cascade & Combo Mechanics

Cascades occur when new tiles fall into place after a match and create additional matches automatically.

Cascades increase the effectiveness of tile effects using a multiplier system.

| Cascade Level | Multiplier |
|---|---|
| Initial Match | 1× |
| First Cascade | 2× |
| Second Cascade | 3× |
| Third Cascade | 4× |

The multiplier applies to all tile effects generated by the cascade, including:

- damage from weapon tiles
- mana generation
- armor generation
- skill charge
- gold gain

Example:

If a weapon match normally deals **10 damage**:

- first cascade → **20 damage**
- second cascade → **30 damage**

This system rewards board planning and encourages players to set up chain reactions.

---

## Extra Turn Rule

Matches of **4 tiles or more** grant an **extra turn**.

Rules:

- 4-match → extra turn
- 5-match → extra turn
- larger matches → extra turn

Extra turns allow the player to perform another full turn (spell phase + tile swap).

Important:

Extra turns **do not generate special tiles**.

This keeps the board mechanics simple for the MVP while still rewarding skilled play.

---

# 7. Combat Turn Structure

A turn consists of **two phases: spell phase and tile phase**.

### Player Turn

1. Player may cast spells (if mana and cooldown allow)
2. Player performs **one tile swap**
3. Matches resolve
4. Cascades resolve
5. Tile effects apply
6. Player turn ends

Important rule:

**Performing a tile swap immediately ends the player's turn.**

# 9A. Attributes

Characters are defined by attributes that influence how puzzle actions translate into combat.

| Attribute | Role | Gameplay Effect |
|---|---|---|
| STR (Strength) | Physical combat | Increases weapon tile damage |
| INT (Intelligence) | Spellcasting | Increases spell damage and spell power |

| VIT (Vitality) | Survivability | Increases maximum health |

Attributes should influence **puzzle interactions**, not only abstract RPG stats.

# 9C. Damage Types

Combat uses a small set of core damage types. Damage types determine how weapons and spells behave and what status effects they can apply.

| Damage Type | Typical Source | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Physical | weapons, melee skills | interacts with armor and can cause bleed |
| Fire | spells, enchanted weapons | may apply burn damage over time |
| Cold | spells | may apply frost / slow effects |
| Lightning | spells | may chain or briefly stun |
| Poison | weapons, abilities | damage over time effect |

Damage types allow enemies to have **resistances or weaknesses**.

Example:

- Skeleton: resistant to poison
- Demon: resistant to fire
- Golem: resistant to lightning

Damage resolution flow:

Base Damage → Attribute Scaling → Damage Type → Enemy Resistance → Status Effects → Final Damage

---

# 9D. Status Effects

Status effects are derived from damage types and remain intentionally simple for the MVP.

| Damage Type | Status Effect | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Fire | Burn | damage over time |
| Cold | Frost | slows enemy actions or reduces damage |
| Lightning | Shock | small chance to stun |
| Poison | Poison | stacking damage over time |
| Physical | Bleed | damage over time that bypasses some armor |

Status effects typically last **2–4 turns** and may stack depending on the source.

---

# 9E. Weapon Archetypes

Weapons determine the base damage for **weapon tile matches** and may apply status effects.

Weapons also have **attribute requirements**, preventing characters from using equipment outside their intended archetype.

| Weapon Type | Typical Damage | Attribute Requirement | Intended Class |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sword | Physical | STR requirement | Warrior |
| Axe | Physical (high damage) | STR requirement | Warrior |
| Dagger | Physical / Poison | DEX requirement | Assassin |
| Bow | Physical / Critical | DEX requirement | Assassin |
| Staff | Elemental spell damage | INT requirement | Wizard |
| Wand | Elemental spell damage | INT requirement | Wizard |

Example requirement:

- Staff requires **INT ≥ 10** to equip

This ensures that:

- STR characters cannot equip spellcasting weapons
- INT characters specialize in magic
- DEX characters favor precision weapons

Weapons may also roll modifiers such as:

- +fire damage
- poison chance
- increased critical chance

# 11. Enemy System

Enemies follow the **same board rules as the player**. They interact with the puzzle board by:

- matching tiles
- generating mana
- gaining armor
- charging skills
- casting spells

Enemies do not generate resources outside the board. Their power comes from stats, abilities, and AI behavior.

## 11.5 Combat Formulas (MVP)

The combat system uses simple formulas to convert puzzle actions into damage and resource gains.

### Weapon Damage

Weapon damage is based on weapon base damage and Strength.

Formula:

Damage = Base Weapon Damage × Tiles Matched × (1 + STR × 0.05)

Example:

Base weapon damage = 8  
STR = 5

3-tile match:

Damage = 8 × 3 × 1.25 = 30

---

### Spell Damage

Spell damage scales with Intelligence.

Formula:

Spell Damage = Base Spell Damage × (1 + INT × 0.05)

---

### Critical Hits

Critical hit chance scales with Dexterity.

Formula:

Crit Chance = Base Crit + (DEX × 0.3%)

Critical hits deal **2× damage**.

---

### Armor Mitigation

Armor reduces incoming physical damage.

Formula:

Final Damage = Incoming Damage − Armor

Armor decays slightly each turn to prevent hoarding.

---

### Damage Resolution Order

Damage is calculated using the following order:

Base Damage  
→ Attribute Scaling  
→ Cascade Multiplier  
→ Enemy Resistance  
→ Status Effects  
→ Final Damage

---

These formulas are intentionally simple for the MVP and can be expanded later as the combat system evolves.
